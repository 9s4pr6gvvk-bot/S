The Reality

Making original
music can be brutal.

It asks a lot of you. Not just creatively, but emotionally. You are expected to make work that is personal, vulnerable and true, then place it in front of the world and somehow stay sane while people ignore it, misunderstand it, admire it, tear it apart or project all sorts onto you.

Add rejection, inconsistency, self-doubt, online bullying, sexism, racism, homophobia and the general strangeness of trying to build something meaningful in public — and it is no wonder so many talented people lose confidence or lose their way.

I know what it is to build something from the ground up, to wear every hat, to keep going when things are difficult, and to try to stay connected to what made you want to do this in the first place.

I'm a singer-songwriter with over ten years of experience fronting a metal band, alongside years of hands-on work in songwriting, singing, performance, arranging, production, branding, marketing and visual direction. I also make all my own music videos.

The Mentorship

More than a
better singer.

My mentorship programmes are not just about becoming a better singer, songwriter or performer. They are about becoming stronger, clearer and more resilient as an artist and as a person.

Becoming the best artist you can be is not just about technical skills — it is about understanding yourself and knowing how to cope when things get hard.

No two artists are the same, so no two packages are the same either. We build your programme together around your strengths, your goals and the areas where you need the most support.
