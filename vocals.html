The Work

Your voice is already
in there.

Voice and performance work isn't about manufacturing a sound or a persona. It's about removing the things that get in the way — the tension, the self-consciousness, the habits that developed from years of singing the wrong way or performing under the wrong conditions.

We work on technique because technique gives you freedom. When you know how your instrument works, you stop thinking about it mid-song. You can be present. You can take risks. You can connect to what you're actually singing about.

Performance work goes further — into how you carry yourself on stage, how you communicate before you've sung a word, and how to build an honest relationship with an audience.

The Approach

How I teach

Over a decade of performing, recording and directing — my teaching comes directly from that experience. Not from a textbook.

01

Technique serves expression

Technical work is only useful insofar as it helps you say something. Everything connects back to the music you're making.

02

Honest feedback, always

I'll tell you what's working and what isn't — clearly and directly. Vague encouragement doesn't help you improve.

03

The whole person

Your mental state, confidence, relationship to performing — these aren't separate from your technique. They're part of it.

04

No cookie-cutter approach

What works for one voice doesn't work for another. Every session is built around where you are and what you need.

05

Long-term development

The goal is skills and awareness that stay with you permanently — so you keep improving long after our sessions end.

06

Real-world experience

Everything I teach comes from having done it: touring, recording, performing original material to audiences who didn't know who I was.
